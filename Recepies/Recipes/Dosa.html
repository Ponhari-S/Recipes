Dosa Recipe

Description:

Dosa is a savory, thin crepe from South India made from a fermented batter of rice and black lentils. It's a popular breakfast or dinner dish, known for its crispy texture and delicious flavor. The key ingredients are rice, black lentils (urad dal), and a small amount of fenugreek seeds (methi), which help in the fermentation process. The preparation involves soaking the rice and lentils for several hours before grinding them into a smooth, thick batter. This batter is then left to ferment overnight, a crucial step that gives the dosa its characteristic sour taste and light texture. Once fermented, the batter is spread thinly on a hot griddle, cooked until golden-brown and crispy, and then folded or rolled. It's typically served with coconut chutney and a warm, spiced lentil-based stew called sambar.

Ingredients:

  • Rice
  • Urad Dal
  • Water
  • Salt
  • Oil
  • Fenugreek Seeds

Process:

  1. Soaking: Combine rice, lentils, and fenugreek seeds in water and let them soak overnight to soften.
  2. Grinding: Blend the soaked ingredients into a smooth, thick, and consistent batter.
  3. Fermentation: Allow the batter to sit in a warm place for 8-12 hours until it rises and develops a sour flavor.
  4. Seasoning: Stir in salt and a little water to the fermented batter to achieve the perfect consistency for cooking.
  5. Cooking: Pour and spread a ladle of batter on a hot griddle to form a thin crepe, cooking until it becomes crispy and golden.
  6. Serving: Immediately serve the hot dosa with traditional accompaniments like sambar and coconut chutney.
